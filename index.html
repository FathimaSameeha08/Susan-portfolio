ABOUT

Ms. Susan Wojcicki is a Chief Executive Officer of YouTube. She serves as Board Member at Salesforce. Wojcicki was instrumental in Google's 2006 acquisition of YouTube. She now oversees YouTube's content and business operations, engineering, and product development. Prior to joining YouTube in February 2014, Wojcicki was senior vice president of Advertising & Commerce at Google, where she oversaw the design and engineering of AdWords, AdSense, DoubleClick, and Google Analytics. She joined Google in 1999 as the company's first marketing manager and led the initial development of several key consumer products, including Google Images and Google Books. In 2002, Wojcicki began working on Google's advertising products and over the next 12 years, she led teams that helped define the vision and direction of Google's monetization platforms. Wojcicki graduated with honors from Harvard University, holds a master's in Economics from the University of California, Santa Cruz, and an MBA from UCLA. She serves as Board Member at Salesforce. She serves as President at Philip And Rebecca Hochman Foundation. She also serves as Treasurer and Secretary at The Anne Wojcicki Foundation.

  • Susan Wojcicki was CEO of Alphabet subsidiary YouTube from 2014 until February 2023. She now serves in an advisory role to Google and Google-parent Alphabet.
  • In 1998, Google cofounders Sergey Brin and Larry Page rented Wojcicki's garage in Menlo Park, California and developed Google's search engine there.
  • She was hired in 1999 as Google employee number 16, and worked on everything from AdSense and Google Analytics to Google Books and Google Images.
  • In 2006, she advocated for the $1.65 billion acquisition of YouTube, which has grown to 2.5 billion monthly viewers.

PROJECTS

YouTube Partner Program

As CEO of YouTube, Wojcicki played a crucial role in the development and expansion of the YouTube Partner Program. This program allows content creators to monetize their videos through advertising and other revenue streams, empowering a vast community of creators and fostering the growth of YouTube as a platform.

YouTube Originals

Wojcicki spearheaded the creation of YouTube Originals, a program that funds and produces original content exclusive to the platform. This initiative aimed to attract traditional Hollywood talent and provide a platform for established and emerging creators to produce high-quality, original content for YouTube's audience.

Content Moderation and Policy Changes

Wojcicki has been involved in implementing and refining content moderation policies on YouTube. She has led efforts to address controversies surrounding inappropriate or harmful content, combat misinformation, and enhance the platform's policies regarding hate speech and harmful behavior.

Expansion of YouTube's Features and Services

Under Wojcicki's leadership, YouTube has expanded its features and services to enhance user experience and meet the changing needs of its audience. This includes the introduction of features such as YouTube Red (later rebranded as YouTube Premium), live streaming capabilities, YouTube TV (a subscription-based streaming service), and YouTube Music.

SERVICES

YouTube Services

As the CEO of YouTube, Wojcicki has overseen the development and management of various services offered by the platform. These include:

  • Video Sharing: YouTube provides a platform for users to upload, share, and discover videos across a wide range of topics.
  • Monetization: YouTube offers services such as the YouTube Partner Program, which allows content creators to monetize their videos through advertising and other revenue streams.
  • Content Discovery: YouTube's recommendation algorithms help users discover relevant and personalized content based on their interests and viewing history.

Marketing Services

During her early years at Google, Wojcicki was involved in the development of advertising and marketing services, which have become integral to Google's revenue streams. These services include:

  • Google AdWords: AdWords is an online advertising platform that enables businesses to create and display ads on Google's search engine results pages and partner websites.
  • Google AdSense: AdSense allows website owners and publishers to earn revenue by displaying targeted ads on their websites or blogs.
  • DoubleClick: DoubleClick is an advertising technology platform that provides tools for advertisers, publishers, and agencies to optimize their digital advertising campaigns.

Educational Initiatives

Wojcicki has supported educational initiatives and services through YouTube. YouTube Learning is an educational initiative that offers a wide range of educational content, tutorials, courses, and resources to facilitate learning and skill development.
